Why This Guide Exists · 02

You don't need another AI tools list.
You need a decision system.

Spending more time evaluating AI tools than building with them is a common trap. Hundreds of options, a new "Top 20" list every week, and deadlines that don't wait.

This guide is a tool-to-task decision system: which tool fits which job, and when to use each one.

AI-Enabled ADDIE · 03

AI-Enabled ADDIE.

Where AI creates leverage across the learning lifecycle

AI conversations often focus on tools. Learning projects focus on outcomes. This view maps AI to the work happening inside each ADDIE phase, helping you identify where AI can reduce effort, accelerate drafting and support better decisions.

ADDIE is a widely used learning design process: Analyse, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate.

ADDIE Stage Common tasks AI tools that help Biggest time-saver
Analyse Gather needs, clarify objectives, understand audience ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, NotebookLM Upload SME docs and let AI extract themes, gaps and audience questions
Design Structure content, outline activities, draft scripts ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Canva Draft outlines, learning objectives, and activity ideas in minutes
Develop Build slides, videos, graphics, eLearning Gamma, Rise, Storyline, HeyGen, Vyond, ElevenLabs, Canva, Descript Build slides, video, audio, eLearning at 3–5× speed
Implement Deploy through LMS, deliver workshops, share videos LMS, 360Learning, video platforms, Camtasia Auto-caption, transcript, distribute via LMS or video platform
Evaluate Collect feedback, analyse results, iterate ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, forms, survey tools, LMS analytics Summarise feedback, suggest improvements, draft survey questions

The same tool can appear at several stages, ADDIE isn't a straight line. Choose by task, not by stage alone. This is a map, not a shackle.

The Tool Selector · 04

The Trainerfy360 Tool Selector.

Four questions that pick the right tool, every time

I was losing hours auditioning every new AI tool instead of building with the ones that work. Four simple questions brought clarity: start with the task, the input, the deadline and the finish, and the right tool usually becomes obvious. AI should support judgement, not replace it.

Answer all four and the shortlist is usually one or two tools. Confirm the fit in the matrix on the next page.

1 · What am I making?
  • Slides → Gamma, Canva or Claude (.pptx)
  • Video → HeyGen, Synthesia or Vyond
  • eLearning → Rise or Storyline
  • Audio → ElevenLabs or Descript
  • Images → Canva AI, Adobe Express, Midjourney, DALL·E 3
  • Documents & surveys → Claude, ChatGPT or Gemini
2 · What's my input?
  • SME notes → Claude, ChatGPT, NotebookLM
  • Recorded footage → Descript, Camtasia
  • A brief or outline → Gamma, Rise, HeyGen
  • A prompt only → Midjourney, DALL·E 3, Vyond
  • Feedback data → ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini
  • Policy documents → NotebookLM, Claude
3 · How much time do I have?
  • Under an hour → Gamma, HeyGen, ElevenLabs
  • Half a day → Rise, Vyond, Descript
  • A full build → Storyline, Camtasia
  • Ongoing → Claude, ChatGPT, NotebookLM
4 · How polished must it be?
  • First draft or internal → Gamma, ChatGPT
  • Client-ready and branded → Canva, Claude
  • Publication-quality → Midjourney, Storyline, Camtasia
  • Compliance content → AI drafts, SME sign-off

From SME Notes to Learning Asset.

What happens between SME input and a finished learning asset is where instructional design expertise creates value.

This is the bit you can't easily Google: a real workflow, from chaos to asset, with decisions and sequencing.

Input → SME notes (Word doc brain dump) · One learning objective · Audience: new managers · Deadline: end of week

Claude, ChatGPT or Gemini
Summarise · Structure · Script
1

Clarify and structure. Upload SME notes + objective + audience; summarise key points and draft the outline and script.

↓
Gamma
Generate slide deck
2

Build the first deck. Paste the outline into Gamma for a clean, structured draft.

↓
Canva
Brand · Polish · Design
3

Brand and refine. Apply the brand kit and templates until it looks polished.

↓
HeyGen or Synthesia
Script → Avatar video · ElevenLabs voice

AI Agents for Training Questions · 10

AI Agents for Training Questions.

Bot and assistant platforms for answering repeated training questions

A fully custom bot isn’t usually the right starting point. Begin with a reliable knowledge-base assistant trained on approved content, and move to custom development only if advanced workflows or tighter integration demand it.

Use case Good options Best for Why it fits
Fast FAQ botJotform AI Agents, Zoho SalesIQ and similar no-code buildersSimple training questions, FAQs, learner supportQuick to set up and easy to maintain
Knowledge-base assistantMicrosoft document Q&A, enterprise knowledge-assistant toolsAnswers grounded in approved policies, guides and training docsBetter control over accuracy and source content
Learning platform botBuilt-in AI agents inside enterprise learning platformsCompliance support, learner guidance, internal workflowsFits the existing learning ecosystem
Custom conversational assistantCustom builds using AI frameworks and APIsBranded assistants, complex workflows, advanced logicHigh flexibility, but needs technical support
Research and synthesis assistantSource-based Q&A tools (notebook-style assistants)Summarising training materials, extracting answers from documentsGreat for internal knowledge and content review

Where to start: treat agents as a faster way to answer repeated training questions from approved content, not as magic. Quick pilot or small team? Start with a no-code bot builder. Enterprise L&D? A knowledge-base assistant or learning-platform bot. Move to a custom build only when you need advanced workflows or deeper integration.
